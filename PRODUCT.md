# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two equal audiences sharing one visit:

**Occasion planners** (moms, brides, event hosts) — they arrive with a specific celebration in mind and need proof Kacey can match their exact theme. They browse the gallery to see range, read the story to decide if they trust her, then click through to order. Confidence in the craft is the conversion.

**Gift buyers** — first-timers who want something personal and memorable for someone they love. They need to feel the process is approachable: not intimidating, not a production commitment. Warmth and simplicity close the sale.

## Product Purpose

Cookies & Crumbs is a one-person custom-cookie bakery based in Broward, FL. Kacey hand-pipes every order to the customer's theme. The site exists to convert traffic from social into bookings — showing work, telling the story, and routing to the Bakesy shop. Success means a visitor who arrived curious leaves with an order submitted or placed in their browser tab.

## Brand Personality

Elegant · Detailed · Personal

Voice is warm but not gushing. Copy is specific: it names the occasion, the craft, the person behind it. No marketing buzzwords. No abstract promises. The work speaks; the copy frames and invites.

## Anti-references

- **Crumbl / mass-market bakery chains**: polished but impersonal, branded sameness, no emotional stake.
- **Generic AI landing page**: blush-cream-sans-serif with eyebrow labels above every section, stock cookie imagery, hero subtext that restates the headline, marquee tickers as filler.
- **Over-produced DTC food brands**: hyper-stylized art direction that feels expensive and distant, not handmade and local.

## Design Principles

1. **The work earns the trust.** Real photography leads every section. Illustrations and abstract placeholders are a last resort, never a design choice.
2. **Personal over polished.** Kacey's voice, her story, and her name appear early. The brand is a person, not a product line.
3. **Specific, not evocative.** "Hand-piped bridal shower set" beats "made with love." Every detail that can be named should be.
4. **Pink, not dusty.** The brand color is a warm bubblegum rose drawn from the circular logo badge — more saturated and clearly pink than a muted mauve or blush. Surfaces lean pink; the deep rose anchors the announcement bar and footer. Cormorant Garamond provides contrast against the warmth. Neither color nor type should be over-applied.
5. **One clear path to order.** Every section resolves to an order action. No dead ends, no ambiguity about what to do next.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Body text must hit ≥4.5:1 against its background. All interactive elements need hover, focus-visible, and active states. Reduce-motion must suppress all transitions without hiding content (no opacity-gated reveals that stay blank). Images require meaningful alt text.
